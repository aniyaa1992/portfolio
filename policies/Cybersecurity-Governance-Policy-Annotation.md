Portfolio Annotation


Who was the audience and under what conditions?


This policy was submitted as a graded, graduate-level academic term project for a cybersecurity governance course at the Georgia Institute of Technology. The intended audience was dual—my course instructor(s) evaluating for technical accuracy and regulatory alignment, and a simulated hospital board and IT security leadership team as the fictional organizational stakeholders. The document was written from scratch. No employer template, existing policy, or institutional framework was provided or adapted.


What problem does this document solve?


The assignment required producing a complete, deployable cybersecurity governance policy for a healthcare organization, addressing ransomware risk across the NIST CSF 2.0 framework domains: Governance, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. The policy needed to satisfy HIPAA Security Rule requirements, HITECH Act guidance, and Indiana state breach notification law, a dual regulatory burden common in real US healthcare environments.


What decisions did I make and why?


I structured the policy around ransomware as the primary threat vector rather than treating it as one risk among many, reflecting the current threat landscape for healthcare organizations. I included a data classification framework with three tiers (Internal, Public, and Confidential) because classification is a prerequisite for applying proportionate controls and is frequently absent from less-mature healthcare security programs. I chose to anchor technical controls explicitly to clinical systems (Pyxis MedStation, MyChart EHR, nurse call systems) rather than generic IT infrastructure because healthcare security policy fails in practice if it ignores the actual clinical technology environment. The four-phase IRP structure (Discovery, Investigation, Response, Closure) was deliberately made brief to function as a governance-level document rather than an operational playbook.


What was my role and scope?


Sole author. All policy language, regulatory cross-referencing, structural decisions, and technical control specifications were written independently. This document was produced as an academic assessment and has not been deployed in a live organizational environment. Hospital name and author identities have been redacted for portfolio use; all policy content is unmodified.


What does this demonstrate?


This document demonstrates my ability to produce governance documentation to professional standards from scratch — without an existing organizational template to adapt. Producing a compliant, well-structured policy from a blank page requires a deeper skill set than reformatting an inherited template. It shows my understanding of the regulatory frameworks being cited, my ability to translate technical controls into enforceable policy language, and my structural judgment to organize a complex compliance domain into a document that is both readable and operationally actionable.
